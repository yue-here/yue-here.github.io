An Illustrated Generative AI Primer

December 18, 2024 · 2 min · 234 words · Yue Wu
Table of Contents

I recently had cause to give a talk about generative AI (‘genAI’), which led me to examine my own understanding. This article is the one I wish I had, giving the intuitions behind generative AI with illustrations and explanations of how things are implemented in code.

Some of the questions I had:

  • What exactly is generative AI and what/how does it learn?
  • Why does generative AI work?
  • How and why do diffusion models work?
  • Once you’ve trained a model, how do you get useful stuff out of it?

I’ll use my implementation of a text-to-image diffusion model (the “Glyffuser”) to explain the following takeaways:

  1. Generative AI aims to learn complex data distributions for sampling
  2. Diffusion models are a powerful framework for learning said distributions
  3. Conditioning enables us to sample meaningful outputs from diffusion models

Generative AI aims to learn complex data distributions for sampling#

Generally, the purpose of a genAI model is to learn the underlying probability distribution of the data. What does this mean? In a simple case, consider the normal (Gaussian) distribution in one dimension. In this case, the probability density function of the distribution is parameterised by the mean and the variance, and we can easily sample from such a distribution in numpy [example code]. (The actual sampling process is non-trivial)
